It’s not gonna matter if you have a few scars. It will matter if you didn’t live.

Rich Mullins

A tribute.

Rich Mullins was a Christian singer-songwriter who’s music and legacy of compassion continues to be influential today. Always uncomfortable in the spotlight, Rich allowed his music to gain worldwide popularity while he retreated from fame, took a vow of poverty, and dedicated the last years of his life to bringing music and the arts to native American children. He was known for the complexity in his music, using unconventional instrumentation and deeply descriptive and metaphorical lyrics. His concerts and writings were filled with his own flavored wisdom, spun from his studies of the Bible and his observations of Christian life.

Highlights of Rich’s life

  • October 21, 1955—Richard Wayne Mullins ("Wayne" to most of his closest friends and family) is born at Reid Memorial Hospital to John A. and Neva Mullins.
  • 1959—Rich’s family discovers his musical talent at around the age of 4 or 5, when his mother hears him play the hymn “Abide With Me” by ear on the family piano.
  • 1971—At age 16, Rich forms his first band, Children of Light, within the all county interdenominational choir New Creations, which he had accompanied on piano since age 13.
  • 1974—After watching the 1972 Franco Zefirelli film Brother Sun, Sister Moon, Rich becomes inspired by the life of St. Francis of Assisi, which would affect the way he approached Christianity for the rest of his life.
  • August 1974—Rich graduates high school and begins the fall semester at Cincinatti Bible College.
  • 1975—Erlanger United Methodist Church in nearby Erlanger, KY, hires Rich Mullins as its youth and music minister.
  • 1978—After working at Erlanger United Methodist Church for three years, Rich leaves to work with Zion Ministries and play in their band, Zion.
  • December 1980—Rich quits Cincinatti Bible College, saying “I had trouble picking out a major.”
  • April 1981—Rich’s band Zion records their first and only LP, Behold the Man, at 5th Floor Studios in Cincinatti, thanks to a $1,000 gift from his uncle. Rich played piano and wrote all of the songs the group performed.
  • July 1981—Behold the Man is released.
  • September 1981—Reunion Records founder Michael Blanton receives a copy of Behold the Man from Zion member Beth Lutz. He immediately falls in love with Rich Mullins‘ song, “Praise to the Lord,” and presents it to Amy Grant and her producer, Brown Banister. The trio begin the process of getting Rich hired as a songwriter.
  • May 1982—Amy Grant’s album Age to Age, which includes “Sing Your Praise to the Lord,” is released. The album claims the #1 spot on the Billboard Top Contemporary Christian Chart, and “Sing Your Praise to the Lord” lands #1 on the Christian AC charts the same year.
  • 1983-1985—Rich works in Nashville as a songwriter, contributing his writing to many Christian artists, including Amy Grant, Debbie Boone and Pam Mark Hall. He is also signed as a recording artist during this time.
  • January 1986—Rich Mullins’ self-titled debut solo album is released.
  • March–May 1986—Rich opens for Amy Grant’s “Unguarded” tour.
  • February 1987—Rich’s second album, Pictures in the Sky, is released.
  • June 1987—“Verge of a Miracle,” from the album Pictures in the Sky, charts #1 on the Christian AC charts. It is Rich Mullins‘ first solo #1 hit.
  • August 1988—Rich’s third album, Winds of Heaven, Stuff of Earth is released. The album is notable for containing the first recording of Rich’s song “Awesome God,” which would become one of the most popular conemporary worship songs in the world.
  • December 1989—Rich’s fourth album, Never Picture Perfect, is released.
  • 1990—Rich begins pursuing a music degree from Friends University in Wichita, KS. He and friend/bandmate David “Beaker” Sasser live in a house together near campus, pooling their resources. Rich instructs his accountant to give him an annual salary of $24,000 per year (the average working person’s wage at the time). The rest of his earnings from album sales, concerts and merchandise would be entrusted to his church for charitable distribution. This was the start of Rich’s Fransican-inspired lifestyle.
  • July 1990—Brennan Manning’s book, The Ragamuffin Gospel, is released. The book would be heavily influential in Rich Mullin’s life.
  • July 1991—Rich’s fifth album, The World as Best as I Remember It, Volume One, is released. The album contains the song “Step by Step,” written by Rich”s longtime friend and collaboratoer Beaker. The song would become a popular worship chorus in its own right.
  • June 1992—The follow-up volume, The World as Best as I Remember It, Volume Two, is released.
  • 1992—Rich travels to Ireland and embarks on a European tour. Many songs for his upcoming album are written while on this tour.
  • 1993—Rich forms the Ragamuffin Band to record his upcoming studio album. The band is named after The Ragamuffin Gospel, written by Brennan Manning.
  • November 1993—Rich’s seventh album, A Liturgy, A Legacy & a Ragamuffin Band, is released. The album is a concept album and becomes a fan favorite.
  • 1994—Rich works with Compassion International, one of the missionary organizations he supports.
  • 1995—Work begins on a musical based on the life of St. Francis, Canticle of the Plains. Rich collaborates with Beaker on the musical, with the lead character Frank inspired by Mitch McVicker, a young songwriter Rich met while attending Friends University. Rich would go on to mentor Mitch until Rich’s death in 1997. It is also around this time Rich refers to his collection of like-minded band members, friends and musicians as “Kid Brothers of St. Frank,” a pseudo-monastic order.
  • May 1995—Rich Mullins graduates from Friends University with a BA in music education. He moves from Wichita, KS to a Navajo Indian reservation in Tse Bonito, NM, to teach music to Navajo children at Hilltop Christian School.
  • July 1995—Brother’s Keeper, Rich’s eighth album, is released.
  • July 1996—Reunion Records releases a “best of” album titled Songs. Included are new recordings of “Screen Door” (originally from Pictures in the Sky) and “Elijah” (originally from Rich Mullins). Also included is “We are not as Strong as We Think We Are,” which was originally written for Brother’s Keeper, but not recorded in time for the album’s release.
  • February 1997—Canticle of the Plains, the musical written by Rich Mullins, Beaker and Mitch McVicker, debuts on KTLI radio in Wichita, KS. The musical is loosely based on the life of St. Francis of Assisi, and involves a character named Frank in the old west.
  • April 1997—Canticle of the Plains has a live performance debut at Wheaton College in Wheaton, IL. Sometime later Rich signs with Myrrh Records.
  • September 1997—Rich gives his studio time in Chicago to Mitch McVicker and helps Mitch record his first album. Rich records demos for his upcoming album on a cassette boombox in an abandoned church near the studio.
  • September 19, 1997—Rich Mullins is killed in a car accident near Bloomington, IL. Rich and Mitch McVicker were on their way to a benefit concert in Wichita, KS, when their Jeep flipped and ejected both men. Mullins was subsequently hit by an oncoming tractor-trailer truck which had swerved to avoid the overturned Jeep. Rich was instantly killed; Mitch survived with serious injuries.
  • April 1998—Rich Mullins is awarded his first Dove Award for “artist of the year.” His brother David and James Dunning, Jr. accept the award on Rich’s behalf.
  • July 1998—Rich Mullins’ final project is released as The Jesus Record, a two-disc set containing the tape-recorded demos Rich had made for the album and studio recordings of the songs made by the Ragamuffin Band with guest artists Michael W. Smith, Amy Grant, Ashley Cleveland and Phil Keaggy.
  • September 2000—James Bryan Smith’s devotional biography about Rich Mullins, titled An Arrow Pointing to Heaven, is released.
  • May 2003—Reunion Records releases a rarities and demos album, Here in America.
  • 2014—An independant film, Ragamuffin, is released to select venues, DVD and Netflix. The film is based on the life of Rich Mullins.
  • April 2014—Rich Mullins is inducted into the Gospel Music Association Hall of Fame.
